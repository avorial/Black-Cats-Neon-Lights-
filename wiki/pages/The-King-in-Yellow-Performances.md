# The King in Yellow (Performances)

## Overview

A performance history of The King in Yellow centered on the [[The Warton Players]], the disastrous 1870 performances, and a third rehearsal at the [[Grand Hotel]].

## Full Text or Extract

The Warton Players performed The King in Yellow twice in 1870. The first performance was praised as a triumph of writing. During the second performance, a fire broke out in the hall and 300 people died. The government declared the writing itself against God and the body politic, and within about three weeks it is believed all copies were destroyed.

The source notes that the first performance coincided with Emperor Napoleon III's capture, while the fire coincided with republicans taking over the country. It also questions how the new government could have gathered all copies so quickly while also trying to root out royalists.

A contact reports a third performance: a dress rehearsal before the opening, not in the main venue but in the ballroom of [[Grand Hotel]], 12 Boulevard des Capucines. Some performers may not have been the same as the main-show performers.
